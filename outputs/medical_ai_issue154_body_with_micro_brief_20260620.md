Small objections are welcome. You do not need clinical data, patient examples, or private material.

### Useful lanes

1. A synthetic example could look like real evidence.
2. A safety sentence could sound stronger than the support behind it.
3. A source link could be mistaken for source support.
4. A reviewer instruction could be unclear.
5. A public release note could sound too ready, too certain, or too broad.

### Boundary

Use synthetic or public examples only.

Do not include patient data, private clinical text, raw private model output, diagnosis advice, treatment advice, clinical validation, clinical deployment, benchmark ranking, score certification, source truth certification, partner claim, institution claim, endorsement, formal application, payment, terms action, budget action, procurement claim, or official role claim.

### Done condition

One outside comment from a third party names a weak spot and gives one safer wording or missing gate.
