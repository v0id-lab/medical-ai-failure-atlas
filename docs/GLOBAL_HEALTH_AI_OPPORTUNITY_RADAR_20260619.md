### Global assurance and benchmark routes

5. The European Commission AI Act page states that AI literacy obligations entered into application on 2 February 2025, GPAI obligations became applicable on 2 August 2025, transparency rules come into effect in August 2026, and more innovators will gain access to regulatory sandboxes including an EU level sandbox.

Source: https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai

Action meaning: sandbox readiness and AI literacy controls should become first class artifacts. The Türkiye assurance lab work should speak in readiness and documentation terms, not clinical validation terms.

6. CHAI lists May 2026 governance playbooks and describes itself as a collaborative learning network for people building, deploying, or using AI in health.

Source: https://www.chai.org/

Action meaning: CHAI is a global language anchor for governance and shared accountability. This does not create affiliation, endorsement, or collaboration. It supports a companion note that maps public atlas artifacts to governance questions.

7. MedHELM describes an open community led benchmark with 121 clinical tasks, 22 subcategories, 31 datasets, and 5 categories.

Source: https://medhelm.org/

Action meaning: the Medical AI Failure Atlas should add benchmark compatibility notes that explain how failure patterns, source support, and clinician review can complement MedHELM style task evaluation without ranking or claiming official compatibility.

8. OpenAI HealthBench describes a health benchmark with 5000 realistic health conversations, physician created rubric criteria, and 262 physicians from 60 countries.

Source: https://openai.com/index/healthbench/

Action meaning: the project should add a HealthBench leakage safe companion note that discusses rubric concepts without copying benchmark examples.

9. BRIDGE describes a multilingual benchmark with 87 clinical text tasks spanning nine languages and more than one million samples, with ongoing leaderboard updates.

Source: https://github.com/YLab-Open/BRIDGE

Action meaning: BRIDGE makes multilingual and cross specialty coverage a priority. The Türkiye track should strengthen Turkish clinical language, specialty spread, source support, and no ranking reporting.
