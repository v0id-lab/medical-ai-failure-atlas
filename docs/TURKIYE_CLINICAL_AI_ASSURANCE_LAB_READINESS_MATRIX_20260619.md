# Türkiye Clinical AI Assurance Lab Readiness Matrix

Date: 2026 06 19

Status: public readiness matrix for a possible Türkiye clinical AI assurance lab route.

Purpose: turn current Türkiye health AI opportunity signals into a concrete readiness matrix for medical AI safety evaluation, clinician AI literacy, health data quality, source support, no ranking reports, and sandbox preparation.

This matrix is not a TÜSEB application, not a TBYS submission, not a TÜBİTAK application, not a PRODİS submission, not a TÜYZE partnership, not a hospital partnership, not an official role, not a clinical validation claim, not clinical deployment, not patient data use, not procurement evidence, not model ranking, not score certification, not payment, and not terms acceptance.

## Readiness matrix

### Lane 1. Route owner and eligibility

Readiness question: who can own the field route, and which live call could actually fit.

Minimum record: route owner, eligibility status, call name, date checked, required portal, first non binding question.

Current state: route owner unknown. TÜSEB A4 UM and TÜBİTAK 1711 are live signals, but neither is treated as a submitted application.

Stop condition: no proposal language until eligibility and route owner are verified.

### Lane 2. Non patient data assurance scope

Readiness question: can the lab start with public examples, synthetic rows, checklists, source support review, and clinician review protocol without patient data.

Minimum record: data source type, privacy boundary, example source, reviewer role, release boundary.

Current state: ready for public non patient data readiness work.

Stop condition: any patient data, hospital data, private operational data, or clinical case material requires a separate decision gate.

### Lane 3. Turkish medical LLM evaluation

Readiness question: what would be evaluated before any public medical AI safety wording is made.

Minimum record: Turkish task surface, specialty lane, source support row, abstention rule, uncertainty rule, no ranking wording.

Current state: ready as a readiness design, not as a model result.

Stop condition: no benchmark score, model rank, or clinical validation statement without reviewed evidence.

### Lane 4. Clinician AI literacy

Readiness question: can a clinician or educator understand what the lab checks before using AI output language.

Minimum record: 30 minute lesson object, blocked claim list, example review row, source support exercise, no patient data boundary.

Current state: ready to package into a hospital or medical faculty literacy packet after route owner response.

Stop condition: do not imply endorsement, adoption, institutional course approval, or required training status.

### Lane 5. Health data quality and label audit

Readiness question: what data quality signals must be visible before any model or benchmark claim is trusted.

Minimum record: label uncertainty, leakage check, source provenance, missing context, disagreement record, reviewer escalation.

Current state: ready as open checklist and audit logic.

Stop condition: do not claim dataset clearance, patient data clearance, or regulatory readiness.

### Lane 6. Governance and no ranking report

Readiness question: does public language separate readiness questions from safety proof.

Minimum record: no ranking boundary, benchmark misuse check, governance intake row, public claim owner, release note.

Current state: ready through the no ranking warning, reviewer handoff card, and hospital governance worksheet.

Stop condition: any phrase that turns a score, leaderboard, fluent answer, or demo into safety proof is blocked.

### Lane 7. Sandbox readiness boundary

Readiness question: what can be prepared before any sandbox, hospital, regulator, or institutional route exists.

Minimum record: route map, assurance lab checklist, risk register, clinical deployment blocker, human review blocker, source support blocker.

Current state: ready for readiness mapping only.

Stop condition: no sandbox access, official role, regulatory evidence, hospital adoption, or deployment readiness claim.

### Lane 8. Opportunity package

Readiness question: what can be shared publicly now so a real route owner can evaluate fit quickly.

Minimum record: one page concept, source anchored matrix, evidence packet index, non patient data boundary, exact decision needed.

Current state: this matrix is the next package.

Stop condition: no formal application, partner commitment, budget, payment, terms acceptance, or institutional role claim without explicit verified route.

## Current opportunity interpretation

1. TÜSEB A4 UM is time sensitive, but eligibility and route owner remain unknown.
2. TÜBİTAK 1711 is live and wider in time, but the public notice frames consortium, customer need, technology provider, and university or public research capacity.
3. The strongest immediate action is not an application. It is a public readiness matrix that makes the route question credible and concrete.
4. If a route owner replies, the smallest next package should be a one page concept with this matrix, no patient data boundary, and exact fit question.
5. If no route owner replies, continue building public assurance lab infrastructure rather than sending repeated cold outreach.

## Minimum pass condition

The assurance lab route is not ready for application language unless route owner, eligibility, source support, data boundary, clinician reviewer role, no ranking language, governance owner, and submission boundary are all recorded.
